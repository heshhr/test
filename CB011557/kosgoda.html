KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERY

History of Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The earliest conservation and research initiative in Sri Lanka began in the beginning of 1978. All seven species of sea turtles at the time were listed as being in risk of extinction. Of the seven species, it was discovered that five of them frequently landed on Sri Lanka's southern coast beaches to deposit their eggs. The people gathered these eggs and sold them in the open market.

Around this time, Victor Hasselblad, a well-known Swedish camera manufacturer whose cameras were the first to be used on the moon, entered the scene. Victor shared similar photographic interests to Dr. Upane de Zylva, a well-known Sri Lankan bird photographer, and the two were good friends. Victor had donated money through Dr. Zylva for use in Sri Lankan conservation and research.

Dr. Siri Wickremesinghe, who passed away, was a close friend of Dr. Zylva. Dr. Wickremesinghe and his companion agreed that a turtle conservation project would be the most effective because they both had a love of nature and wild animals. Similiyas Abbrew, the father of Chandrasiri and the grandfather of Isuru, just so happened to be a friend of Dr. Zylva's, and when he learned of their intended endeavor, he was more than willing to donate a portion of his land to construct the hatchery. It all came together because Nape in Kosgoda was a favorite beach for turtles to deposit their eggs. The turtles have found a secure habitat and are multiplying as a result of Victor Hasselblad, Dr. Zylva, and Dr. Wickremesinghe. The hatchlings running to catch the first wave after being released on the shore is a magnificent sight to behold.

About Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, located in Sri Lanka, is a prominent conservation center dedicated to protecting and preserving sea turtles. Established with the aim of addressing the declining population of these endangered creatures, the hatchery focuses on safeguarding turtle eggs, nurturing hatchlings, and promoting awareness about sea turtle conservation. Through its efforts, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery has become a vital institution in Sri Lanka's conservation landscape, playing a crucial role in ensuring the survival of sea turtles and fostering a sense of environmental responsibility among visitors and the local community.

Sea Turtles

Green Turtle

These turtles are known to nest in Akurella, Mawella, Kosgoda, Rekawa, Ambalantota, Yala & Bundala of Sri Lanka. They are widely distributed in tropical & sub tropical regions especially around oceanic islands & along coasts with wide sandy beaches. Also they are seen in all oceans with primarily temperate waters. There is a huge population in Atlantic & Eastern Pacific oceans.

Hawksbill Turtle

Nesting sites of Sri Lanka includes Kataduwa, Bentota, Udappuwa, Kosgoda, Karaduwa, Talaimannar, Jaffna, Galkissa, Talawila, Palatupana, Amadhauwa. These turtles are found mainly in the tropical regions of the Atlantic, Pacific, Indian Oceans & Mediterranean Sea. They are most associated with tropical waters.

Loggerhead Turtle

They are found in south & south-east shore of Sri Lanka including Kosgoda & Rakawa. But they are also seen very rarely in Sri Lanka. This turtle is found in nearly all the world's temperate and tropical oceans: the Atlantic Ocean from Newfoundland to Argentina, the Indian Ocean from southern Africa to the Arabian Gulf to Western Australia, the Mediterranean Sea, and the Pacific Ocean from Alaska to Chile and Australia to Japan. During winter months loggerhead sea turtles migrate to tropical and subtropical waters

Olive Ridley Turtle

In Sri Lanka this species is known to nest in Karaduwa, Maggona, Bundala, MArichchikaddi, Kalpitiya, Udappuwa, Hambantota, Kundakuliya, Chilaw, Negambo, MAtara, Weligama. They are common in the Bay of Bengal, seen especially along parts of the Tamil Nadu coastline. They generally tend to stay within the latitudes of 40° North and 40° south. It’s usually found in large range within the tropical and subtropical regions in the Indo-Pacific and southern Atlantic oceans, beaches of Orissa, India, Chennai, Saint Martin's Island in Bangladesh, Caribbean Sea and along the Gulf of California. It is also known as the Pacific Ridley

Leatherback Turtle

These turtles are seen nesting in Sri Lanka including, Induruwa, Mawella, Ussangoda, Rekawa, Ambalangoda, Kosgoda, Bundala, Godavaya & Yala. Although leatherbacks are most often found in tropical waters, they are distributed around the globe in temperate oceans, and even on edges of sub arctic water & sub arctic oceans. They travel north & live in Northern Atlantic waters as far north as Newfoundland, Nova Scotia, and Labrador. They also inhabit South Atlantic Waters, as far south as Argentina and South Africa. This turtle inhabits waters as far east as Britain and Norway.

Turtle Hatchery Sri Lanka

The turtle hatcheries of Sri Lanka are vital conservation centers that play a crucial role in protecting and preserving the country's endangered turtle species. These hatcheries provide a safe and controlled environment for sea turtles to lay their eggs and ensure the survival of their offspring.
