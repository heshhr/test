HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY

About Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Sea Turtle Hatchery & Rescue Center, a modest yet lovely turtle farm and rescue facility, is located in Hikkaduwa. Visitors may view turtle eggs that are securely buried in the hatchery's sand here. Eggs typically hatch after two days. To ensure that they develop properly and may subsequently be returned to the water safely, where they can live in their native habitat, turtles are handled very delicately. Since turtles must develop in order to be released and continue living on their own, visitors may witness turtles of a variety of sizes here.

Visitors will also regrettably be able to witness turtles that have had body parts amputated, such as those missing forelegs or hind legs. The usual source of this physical injury is boat engines. Turtles lose their balance after losing a leg, and it takes a while for them to recover and regain complete body control as it was before the tragedy. This is why the farm is also regarded as a special place where handicapped turtles may rediscover balance in their bodies and continue to live.

In the past, many turtles perished as a result of unintentionally ingesting plastic bags, which they mistook for jellyfish. Recently, the Sri Lankan government enacted harsh penalties for anyone who throw plastic bags into the ocean. The precautions were effective, and there has recently been a decrease in the number of turtles who die in this manner. The turtle farm in Hikkaduwa is another location where visitors may see the turtles, get to know them, and discover a lot about these majestic creatures. The tour guides genuinely increase people's understanding of the value of marine life and all creatures that reside there.

Turtle Hatchery Sri Lanka

The turtle hatcheries of Sri Lanka are vital conservation centers that play a crucial role in protecting and preserving the country's endangered turtle species. These hatcheries provide a safe and controlled environment for sea turtles to lay their eggs and ensure the survival of their offspring.

About The Hatchery

Of the seven living sea turtle species recorded in the world, five species were reported in the coastal belt of Sri Lanka coming for nesting. The south and southwest coastline of Sri Lanka comprises the largest marine turtle rookeries.
